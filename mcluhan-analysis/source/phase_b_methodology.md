# Phase B: Analysis Methodology

## Overview

Each spread in *The Medium is the Massage* receives a structured JSON entry per `analysis_schema_v1.1.json`. This document describes the methodology used for each field, the tools involved, and guiding questions for the interpretive sections (Themes & Progression) that require the most analytical rigor.

---

## Field-by-Field Methodology

| Field | Primary Method | Tool(s) | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|

### VLM Correlation Strategy

For maximum robustness, image descriptions and layout analyses are generated by **two independent VLMs**:

1. **Qwen2.5-VL-7B** (via Ollama, `qwen2.5vl:7b`): Primary OCR + image description + layout analysis
2. **Molmo2-8B** (via HuggingFace Transformers 4.57.1, conda env `molmo`): Secondary image description + layout analysis for correlation

Where the two models agree, confidence is high. Where they diverge, Claude performs manual arbitration by viewing the spread image directly.

**Potential upgrade**: Qwen3-VL is now available on Ollama and may offer improved accuracy. Consider evaluating for batch 2+.

## Guiding Questions: Themes

The `themes` section maps each spread's content to both original thematic concerns and contemporary AI-era domains. This is the most forward-looking analytical work.

### `original_themes` (free-text tags)

Ask yourself:
1. **What is this spread fundamentally about?** Not the surface topic, but the underlying concern. A spread about TV is really about passivity, or participation, or the blurring of public and private.
2. **What recurring McLuhan concern does this address?** (e.g., sensory ratios, extensions of man, hot vs. cool media, figure-ground, the global village, obsolescence, retrieval)
3. **Would someone reading only this spread understand what McLuhan is arguing?** If not, what theme connects it to the broader argument?
4. **Use consistent vocabulary across all 85 spreads.** Before assigning a theme, check what tags have been used on previous spreads. Prefer existing tags over inventing new ones unless the concept is genuinely new.

**Common theme vocabulary** (non-exhaustive, expand as needed):
`privacy`, `surveillance`, `education`, `the_child`, `speed`, `environment`, `technology_as_extension`, `authorship`, `electric_age`, `participation`, `obsolescence`, `perception`, `the_body`, `sensory_experience`, `disruption`, `transformation`, `mediation`, `identity`, `the_individual_vs_the_collective`, `figure_ground`, `hot_and_cool_media`, `print_culture`, `acoustic_space`, `visual_space`

### `contemporary_domain_candidates` (enum values)

For each spread, ask:
1. **Which of the 12 domains does this spread's argument map to most naturally?**
   - `algorithmic_identity` ‚Äî How AI/algorithms construct, classify, and define us
   - `attention_and_cognition` ‚Äî How technology reshapes how we think, focus, remember
   - `synthetic_media_and_post_truth` ‚Äî Deepfakes, AI-generated content, epistemic crisis
   - `ambient_intelligence` ‚Äî IoT, smart environments, always-on computation
   - `post_literacy_and_language` ‚Äî LLMs, voice interfaces, death of reading/writing as we know it
   - `authorship_and_creativity` ‚Äî AI art, generative models, who creates?
   - `surveillance_and_control` ‚Äî Data harvesting, facial recognition, algorithmic governance
   - `public_private_collapse` ‚Äî Social media, always-on visibility, loss of interiority
   - `embodiment_and_disembodiment` ‚Äî VR/AR, telepresence, the body in digital space
   - `labor_and_value` ‚Äî Automation, gig economy, AI replacing human work
   - `global_village_revisited` ‚Äî Interconnection, filter bubbles, digital tribalism
   - `agency_and_autonomy` ‚Äî Who decides? Human vs. algorithmic choice
2. **Is there a direct conceptual bridge?** Don't force a mapping. Some spreads (especially transitional/structural ones) may have empty domain candidates.
3. **Could this spread belong to multiple domains?** If so, list all that apply. The goal is to build a rich thematic map, not force one-to-one correspondence.
4. **Does the mapping feel earned or forced?** A good mapping makes the reader think "of course" ‚Äî the connection illuminates both the original and the contemporary. A forced mapping makes the reader think "really?"

### `movement_mapping` (enum values)

Assign the spread to one (or occasionally two) of the contemporary book's structural movements:

- **`prologue`** ‚Äî Framing, thesis statement, setting the terms. Spreads 001-008 typically.
- **`movement_1_environment`** ‚Äî "The Medium as Environment." How technology becomes our invisible surround. Surveillance, ambient intelligence, the infrastructure we swim in.
- **`movement_2_acceleration`** ‚Äî "Speed, Scale, Saturation." The pace, volume, and overwhelm of the AI age. Attention collapse, information overload, time compression.
- **`hinge_m2_m3`** ‚Äî Transitional spreads that pivot from the analysis of problems (M1-M2) toward the visionary/dreamlike territory of M3.
- **`movement_3_dreamscape`** ‚Äî "The Dreamscape of Possibility." What comes after the disruption? New forms of literacy, creativity, identity, agency.

Ask:
1. **Is this spread primarily diagnostic (what's happening) or generative (what could happen)?** Diagnostic ‚Üí M1 or M2. Generative ‚Üí M3.
2. **Does this spread describe the environment or the speed?** Environment ‚Üí M1. Speed/overwhelm ‚Üí M2.
3. **Does this spread feel like a beginning, middle, or end?** Beginning ‚Üí Prologue. Middle (problem) ‚Üí M1/M2. Middle (pivot) ‚Üí Hinge. End (vision) ‚Üí M3.

### `mapping_rationale`

This is the **conceptual bridge** ‚Äî the sentence or two that explains why the mapping works. It should:
1. Name the specific original concern
2. Name the specific contemporary parallel
3. Explain the conceptual link between them
4. Be concise but substantive (2-4 sentences)

**Good example** (from gold standard spread_011):
> McLuhan's 'computerized dossier bank' maps directly to today's algorithmic profiling, data brokerage, and AI-driven surveillance systems. The 'womb-to-tomb surveillance' he describes in 1967 has been realized in ways he could not have imagined ‚Äî browser histories, location tracking, facial recognition, social media graphs.

**Weak example** (too vague):
> This spread is about technology and privacy, which is still relevant today.

## Guiding Questions: Progression

The `progression` section tracks how the book builds its argument sequentially. Each spread is a beat in a rhythm.

### `pace_shift`

Ask:
1. **Does this spread feel faster or slower than the previous one?** Consider: amount of text, visual density, image complexity, the demand on the reader's attention.
2. **Is there a sudden break in rhythm?** A full-bleed photograph after pages of dense text = `rupture`. A continuation of the same style = `steady`.
3. **How much time does the reader spend here?** A quick typographic splash = `accelerating`. A dense paragraph requiring careful reading = `decelerating`.

### `thematic_function`

Ask:
1. **Is this the first time this idea appears?** ‚Üí `introduces_theme`
2. **Does this build on a theme already introduced?** ‚Üí `develops_theme`
3. **Does this combine multiple previously-introduced themes?** ‚Üí `layers_themes`
4. **Is this a peak moment of intensity or confrontation?** ‚Üí `climax`
5. **Does this ease tension or provide closure?** ‚Üí `resolution`
6. **Is this a bridge between different thematic sections?** ‚Üí `transition`
7. **Does this echo or revisit something from earlier?** ‚Üí `coda`
8. **Does this break the flow entirely?** ‚Üí `interruption`

### `relationship_to_previous`

Be **specific** ‚Äî reference the actual content of the previous spread.

**Good**: "Shifts from the intimate close-up of hands (spread_007) to the stark typographic confrontation of 'and how!' ‚Äî moving from sensory/tactile mode to declarative/verbal mode."

**Weak**: "Continues from the previous spread."

Ask:
1. **What was the reader experiencing on the previous spread?** An image? Dense text? A visual joke? Silence?
2. **How does THIS spread change that experience?** Continuation, escalation, contrast, pivot, relief?
3. **Is there a visual or thematic rhyme?** Same color palette? Same theme, different angle?

### `relationship_to_next`

Be **specific** about what the next spread introduces. If you haven't analyzed the next spread yet, note what you expect based on the current spread's setup.

**Good**: "The exclamation sets up the exploratory mode that follows ‚Äî having established that 'advances wreck societies,' the book proceeds to inventory the wreckage."

**Weak**: "Sets up the next spread."

Ask:
1. **What does this spread leave the reader wanting?** More information? Resolution? Contrast?
2. **What expectation does it create?** If the spread asks a question, the next spread likely answers it (or deliberately doesn't).
3. **Is there a cliffhanger, open loop, or setup?**

## Quality Checklist (Per Entry)

Before finalizing each entry, verify:

- [ ] `body_text` matches OCR extraction (spot-check against image)
- [ ] `display_text` accurately captures large/prominent text
- [ ] `quotations` have correct attribution and source identification
- [ ] `images` descriptions match what's visible in the spread
- [ ] `source_credit` cross-referenced against `image_credits_lookup.json`
- [ ] `rhetoric.argument` is substantive (not just a description of content)
- [ ] `rhetoric.design_enacts_argument` is `true` only when design genuinely performs the argument
- [ ] `themes.original_themes` use consistent vocabulary
- [ ] `themes.contemporary_domain_candidates` have an earned mapping (not forced)
- [ ] `themes.mapping_rationale` names specific original AND contemporary parallels
- [ ] `progression.relationship_to_previous/next` reference specific content (not generic)
- [ ] `notes` include any cross-references, credits, or uncertainties
- [ ] No encoding artifacts (em dashes should be U+2014, not garbled)
