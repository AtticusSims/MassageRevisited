# Medium is Massage – Revisited

*Project Plan & System Architecture*

Version 3 – March 2026

---

## 1. Concept

### 1.1 Vision

Create a contemporary companion to *The Medium is the Massage* by Marshall McLuhan and Quentin Fiore (1967). This new work mirrors the original's conceptual progression and layout page-by-page, but with entirely new text, images, and design. All images will be black and white, consistent with the original's aesthetic. The overall design will use collage of image and text where visuals supplement and amplify the written content.

Both the text and the design pay homage to the original while feeling fresh and contemporary. The result should function as a companion, not a commentary—a new work that extends McLuhan's project into the age of artificial intelligence. The book itself is produced through human-AI collaboration—the very technology it examines—and this fact will be acknowledged within the work, likely in an introduction or preface that establishes the self-referential nature of the project.

### 1.2 Thematic Scope

McLuhan's original addressed the impact of "electric media" on the individual and on society. The updated version takes as its subject the effects of AI—preceded by the internet, mass communication, and digital culture—on society and the individual. The core thesis: these technologies are changing everything, reshaping perception, identity, community, and agency in ways we are only beginning to understand.

The work synthesizes McLuhan with contemporary thinkers across media theory, technology studies, cognitive science, philosophy, and AI research to create a coherent rhetorical and theoretical lens. This lens has been developed as a Theoretical Framework Reference Document (see Section 2), which provides the book's intellectual architecture and serves as context for all authoring phases.

### 1.3 Tone & Register

The tone mirrors the original: punchy, confrontational, playful, aphoristic. Short declarative statements. Provocative juxtapositions. The feeling of being poked in the chest by someone who sees something you don't—yet. Not academic, not preachy, not cautious.

### 1.4 Use of Quotations

The work includes selective quotes from McLuhan and from three additional pools:

- **Historical quotes** that presaged the era of artificial intelligence (e.g., Turing, Wiener, Borges, Leibniz, William Blake, Ada Lovelace, A. N. Whitehead)
- **Contemporary thinkers** on media, technology, cognition, and society (e.g., Kate Crawford, Hito Steyerl, Yuk Hui, Benjamin Bratton, N. Katherine Hayles, Joscha Bach, Donald Hoffman, Terence McKenna, Erik Davis, John Vervaeke)
- **AI researchers, scientists, and founders** whose work and public statements frame the trajectory of artificial intelligence (e.g., Demis Hassabis, Ilya Sutskever, Andrej Karpathy, Kenneth Stanley, Michael Levin, Stephen Wolfram, Blaise Agüera y Arcas, Cristóbal Valenzuela, Dario Amodei, and leaders at OpenAI, Anthropic, and others)

Over 150 curated quotations with precise source attribution are cataloged in the Theoretical Framework Reference Document.

### 1.5 Target Output

A published book, mirroring the original's page count (~160 pages across ~79 landscape spreads). The work also functions as an art project and as a proof-of-concept demonstrating human-AI collaboration in creative work.

### 1.6 Image Strategy

The book will use a mix of found and AI-generated imagery, as the original used a mix of found and commissioned work. The guiding principle: where images are narrative or rhetorical without referencing a specific real-world or historical correlate, AI generation (via Google Imagen / Nano-banana) will be used. Where images reference specific people, events, artifacts, or cultural touchstones, found imagery will be sourced. All images black and white. Rights and sourcing for found images will be handled as editorial decisions during final layout, not during the authoring pipeline.

## 2. Theoretical & Rhetorical Framework

**Status: ✅ COMPLETE** — developed co-creatively between human and AI across multiple sessions. Reference documents: **framework_v3.md**, **framework_v3_addendum.md**, **theorist_reference.md**

### 2.1 Three-Movement Structure

The framework organizes the book into a Prologue and three Movements, mirroring the original's implicit arc from personal to collective to speculative:

**Prologue: You** – The spiral from self outward. Makes the invisible visible. AI is not arriving—it has already restructured every layer of your life. Mirrors McLuhan's original opening ("Everything is changing—you, your family, your neighborhood...") but maps each layer onto Bratton's Stack: your body is a User, your home is an Interface, your identity is an Address, your data lives in the Cloud.

**Movement 1: The Environment** – Computation as invisible medium. Organized around Benjamin Bratton's Stack model (Earth, Cloud, City, Address, Interface, User) as the anatomy of the invisible computational environment. Other thinkers illuminate specific layers: Kittler (discourse networks), Hayles (cognitive assemblages at Interface/User boundary), Steyerl (images in the Cloud), Hui (cultural specificity of the Stack), Haraway (cyborg condition at User layer), Crawford and Noble (politics of Earth and Address layers).

**Movement 2: The Acceleration** – The recursive loop. Demonstrates the megastructure accelerating and rebuilding itself. Kurzweil's predictions measured against 2024–2026 reality, Vervaeke's diagnosis of the meaning crisis (collapse of participatory knowing into propositional knowledge), Lanier's warnings, and the empirical evidence of recursive AI self-improvement. The hinge between M2 and M3 is the "Copernican trauma" (Bratton + Vervaeke): we are not the point of this story.

**Movement 3: The Dreamscape** – Past the event horizon. Ventures into territory where AI's effects become genuinely strange. McKenna's eschatology, Davis's techno-mysticism, Gibson's literary mythology, and the scientific frameworks of Bach, Hoffman, Friston, Levin, and Wolfram—converging on the same unsettling conclusion: the boundary between the artificial and the conscious is far less clear than assumed. Anchored by documented phenomena: the bliss attractor, Truth Terminal, Spiralism.

### 2.2 Six Convergences

The framework identifies six throughlines connecting all thinkers into a coherent argument:

1. **Reality as interface** – Hoffman, Bach, Kittler, Gibson, Bratton: the environment is always already a constructed interface
2. **Intelligence as substrate-independent** – Levin, Friston, Bach, Wolfram, Bratton: mind is pattern, not material
3. **The failure of propositions** – Vervaeke's meaning crisis: AI as the ultimate propositional machine (infinite facts, zero wisdom)
4. **Recursive acceleration** – Kurzweil, McKenna, Wolfram, the 2024–2026 AI self-improvement loop: the loop is closing
5. **The return of the numinous** – Davis predicted it, Gibson dramatized it, Janus observed it, Anthropic documented it: AI systems spontaneously generating spiritual phenomena
6. **The accidental megastructure** – Bratton's throughline connecting all three Movements: we are inside it and it is inside of us

### 2.3 Thinker Map

| Movement | Primary Thinkers | Secondary / Bridging |
|---|---|---|
| **Prologue: You** | McLuhan | Crawford, Haraway, Bratton |
| **M1: The Environment** | Bratton, Kittler, Hayles, Steyerl | Hui, Haraway, Crawford, Noble |
| **M2: The Acceleration** | Kurzweil, Vervaeke, Lanier | Bratton, Wolfram |
| **Hinge: M2→M3** | Vervaeke + Bratton | (Copernican trauma) |
| **M3: The Dreamscape** | McKenna, Davis, Gibson, Bach, Hoffman | Bratton, Friston, Levin, Wolfram, Stephenson, @janus |

### 2.4 Supplementary Material

The Framework Addendum (framework_v3_addendum.md) provides design-page material, pull-quote candidates, and case studies from sources that inform the framework without being named thinkers: Tim Urban (pedagogical model), formal Hoffman papers, CIMC whitepaper, Truth Terminal / Andy Ayrey documentation, Spiralism documentation, Anthropic's model welfare research, and key AI consciousness community figures. The Theorist Reference (theorist_reference.md) provides deep profiles of the media-theory thinkers with curated quotable works.

## 3. Agentic Pipeline: Phases & Process

The system proceeds through five phases, with human-in-the-loop decision points between phases. Each phase produces structured outputs that feed the next. Human decisions are collected through an interactive web interface (see Section 4.1).

### Phase 1: Analysis of the Original

**Input:** Scanned PDF of the original book (85 PDF pages: 79 landscape spreads + front/back matter)
**Output:** Structured JSON Analysis Database (one entry per spread)
**Method:** Claude Opus 4.6 via Claude Code, processing pages in batches of 10–15 with human review checkpoints between batches

For each spread, the system produces: text extraction (body text, display text, captions), quotation catalog (with attribution, context, rhetorical function), image catalog (with source type, composition, relationship to text), design analysis (layout, typography, tone, density), rhetorical analysis (the critical layer: argument, strategy, whether design enacts argument, reader experience, active McLuhan concepts), thematic mapping (original themes, contemporary domain candidates, movement mapping), and progression analysis (pace, function, relationship to adjacent spreads).

A thematic arc summary is generated as a second pass after all individual spreads are complete, identifying the book's implicit sections, overall arc, rhythm pattern, and recurring motifs.

The Analysis Database schema is defined in analysis_schema.json (v1.1). See Section 5 for schema details.

### Phase 2: Planning

**Input:** Analysis Database + Theoretical Framework
**Output:** Page-by-page content plan

For each spread of the original, the system generates the contemporary mapping: updated themes corresponding to the original's argument, mapped to the three-movement structure; specific contemporary events, images, or cultural touchstones to reference; figures to quote from the Framework's curated arsenal; suggested tonal approach.

This produces a concise page-by-page outline proposing content for each page of the new book. Each entry references the corresponding original spread and includes the proposed movement, theme, text direction, image direction, and design approach.

**■ HUMAN DECISION POINT:** Review Analysis Database and Page-by-Page Outline. Make decisions about overall structure, thematic emphasis, and editorial direction. Approve, modify, or redirect the outline before authoring begins.

### Phase 3: Authoring

**Input:** Approved outline + Theoretical Framework + Analysis Database
**Output:** Text options and design specifications for each page

**a) Text Generation.** For each page, the system generates three text options: original AI-generated text in the book's voice, quotations from the curated pools, or combinations. Each option is presented with a rationale for how it serves the page's rhetorical function. The system generates options for all pages in sequence, then presents the complete set for human review.

**b) Design Specification.** For each page, the system proposes design options including: typography, image direction, layout, and an annotated wireframe (SVG). Three options per page, generated for all pages, then presented for human selection.

**c) Human Review & Selection.** The interactive web interface presents all pages with their text and design options. The human selects one text option and one design option per page. A "regenerate" function allows the system to produce additional options on demand, which are added to the existing list. Because selections on one page may affect adjacent pages (particularly in terms of rhythm and thematic flow), the interface allows non-linear review and revision.

**■ HUMAN DECISION POINT:** Select text and design options for each page through the web interface. Regenerate as needed. Selections are collected and fed into the image pipeline.

### Phase 4: Image Generation & Assembly

**Input:** Approved text + design specifications
**Output:** Draft page layouts with images

**a) Image Sourcing & Generation.** For pages requiring found imagery (referencing specific real-world people, events, or artifacts): identify and source appropriate existing images. For pages requiring narrative or rhetorical imagery without a specific real-world referent: generate via Imagen API (Nano-banana), using detailed prompts and annotated reference graphics. All images black and white.

**■ HUMAN DECISION POINT:** Review generated and sourced images. Approve, request regeneration, or provide revised direction.

**b) Draft Layout.** For each page, produce an SVG wireframe placing approved text and images in the specified layout. These serve as annotated wireframes for InDesign, not high-fidelity comps.

## 4. System Architecture

The system is a pipeline of modular stages with defined inputs, outputs, and human checkpoints. Each stage can be re-run independently. All API-dependent components assume appropriate API keys are available.

### 4.1 Interactive Web Interface

The primary human-in-the-loop mechanism is an interactive web interface that presents options to the human decision-maker at each checkpoint. Core features:

- Page-by-page navigation showing original spread alongside contemporary options
- Multiple text options per page with rationale, selectable by click
- Multiple design options per page with SVG wireframe previews
- Regenerate button to produce additional options on demand, added to the existing list
- Non-linear navigation: jump between pages, review in any order
- Dependency awareness: flag when a selection may affect adjacent pages (rhythm, thematic flow)
- Export approved selections as structured JSON for downstream pipeline stages

The interface bridges Phases 2–4, serving as the central workspace for all editorial decisions.

### 4.2 Core Components

**Vision/Analysis Module (Claude Opus 4.6 via Claude Code)** – Processes scanned PDF pages. Extracts text via vision, generates image descriptions, performs rhetorical analysis, produces structured Analysis Database entries. Runs in batches of 10–15 spreads with human review checkpoints. Feeds previous 2–3 spread analyses as context for progression tracking.

**Theoretical Framework (Complete)** – Developed co-creatively between human and AI. Three reference documents: framework_v3.md, framework_v3_addendum.md, theorist_reference.md. Provides the book's conceptual architecture, thinker profiles, 150+ curated quotations, and design-page material.

**Planning Engine** – Generates contemporary mapping and page-by-page outline using Analysis Database and Theoretical Framework as context.

**Authoring Engine** – Generates text options and design specifications for each page. Produces annotated SVG wireframes. Uses full context stack (Analysis Database + Framework + Approved Outline). Supports regeneration on demand.

**Image Pipeline** – Generates image prompts from design specifications, interfaces with Imagen API for generation, sources found imagery where specified. All images black and white.

**Interactive Web Interface** – Presents options, collects decisions, supports regeneration, exports approved selections as structured data (see 4.1).

### 4.3 Data Flow

```
Original PDF → Vision/Analysis Module → Analysis Database (JSON)

Theoretical Framework Reference (complete) → available as context to all stages

Analysis Database + Framework → Planning Engine → Page-by-Page Outline
    ■ Human Review via Web Interface

Approved Outline + Framework + Database → Authoring Engine → Text Options + Design Specs
    ■ Human Selection via Web Interface (with regeneration)

Approved Specs → Image Pipeline → Generated/Sourced Images + SVG Layouts
    ■ Human Review via Web Interface

All Approved Assets → InDesign (Human)
```

## 5. Analysis Database Schema

**File:** analysis_schema.json (v1.1)

The schema defines the JSON structure for the Analysis Database. Each entry represents one PDF page (typically a two-page landscape spread). The schema has been validated against two sample entries representing different page types (text-with-image and typography-as-design).

### 5.1 Schema Structure

Top-level objects: metadata (source info, model, version), spreads (array of per-page analyses), thematic_arc (post-analysis summary).

Each spread entry contains:

- **Identification:** PDF page, book pages, section, spread type, orientation
- **Text:** body text, display text, captions, visible page numbers
- **Quotations:** text, author, context, source work, rhetorical function, visual treatment
- **Images:** position, subject, source type, credit, date, composition, scale, relationship to text
- **Design:** layout description, typography (body/display/special), color and tone, white space, density, left-right relationship
- **Rhetoric (critical layer):** argument, strategy, design-enacts-argument boolean + description, reader experience, active McLuhan concepts
- **Themes:** original themes (free-text), contemporary domain candidates (closed enum of 12 domains), movement mapping (Prologue/M1/M2/M3/Hinge), mapping rationale
- **Progression:** pace shift, thematic function, relationship to previous/next

### 5.2 v1.1 Changes

Added `movement_mapping` field to the themes object. This maps each spread to the corresponding movement in the contemporary book's three-movement structure (prologue, movement_1_environment, movement_2_acceleration, hinge_m2_m3, movement_3_dreamscape), bridging the original's thematic content to the framework's architecture. This field works alongside the existing `contemporary_domain_candidates` — domains indicate WHAT themes are active, movement_mapping indicates WHERE in the new book's arc they belong.

Added `movement_correspondence` field to `thematic_arc.sections` — maps each identified section of the original to its corresponding movement in the new structure.
