## 1. Wait But Why — Tim Urban

**Source:** "The AI Revolution" (Parts 1–2, January 2015), "Elon Musk Series" (Posts 1–4, May–November 2015), "Neuralink and the Brain's Magical Future" (April 2017). All at waitbutwhy.com.

**Framework status:** Not a named thinker. Urban is a translator/popularizer — the best of his generation — but his ideas originate with Kurzweil, Bostrom, and others already in the framework. His value to the book is rhetorical and structural, not theoretical.

**Why it matters for the book:** Urban's visual-explanation method (stick figures, "tree trunks," fog-clearing) is actually a model for how the book itself should onboard readers. His massive readership (the AI Revolution posts were read by tens of millions) is evidence that people hunger for the "tree trunk" the framework's thinkers provide. The cook/chef distinction maps directly onto the McLuhan fish-in-water problem.

### Pull Quotes and Design-Page Candidates

**The Cook and the Chef (Movement 1 — making the invisible visible):**

> "When it comes to most of the way we think, the way we make decisions, and the way we live our lives, we're much more like the flood geologists than the science geologists."
> — Tim Urban, "The Cook and the Chef: Musk's Secret Sauce," Wait But Why, November 6, 2015

*Use: Design page in Movement 1. The cook/chef binary is the reader's own situation — the cook doesn't know they're in water; the chef has somehow noticed. This is McLuhan's central insight rendered in stick-figure language.*

> "A chef reasons from first principles. A cook follows a recipe. The difference is not intelligence — it's the willingness to examine whether your recipe is based on reality."
> — Tim Urban, "The Cook and the Chef," Wait But Why, November 6, 2015 (paraphrased from extended passage)

*Use: Epigraph candidate for Movement 1 or Prologue.*

**The Tree Trunk metaphor (Movement 1 — structural understanding):**

> "If you don't fully get it, it's like a tree in your head with no trunk — and without a trunk, when you learn something new about the topic — a new branch or leaf of the tree — there's nothing for it to hang onto, so it just falls away."
> — Tim Urban, "How Tesla Will Change The World," Wait But Why, June 2, 2015

*Use: Design page or sidebar. This describes what the book is trying to build in the reader's mind — the tree trunk of understanding about computational infrastructure that makes all subsequent AI news comprehensible.*

**Energy as invisible infrastructure (Movement 1 — Earth layer of the Stack):**

The Tesla post's deep excavation of energy infrastructure — fossil fuels as stored solar joules, the entire energy economy as a system of theft and extraction — does for the physical substrate what Bratton does for the computational substrate. Urban builds a "tree trunk" where energy infrastructure becomes visible as infrastructure for the first time.

*Use: Not a direct quote but a structural parallel. If the book has pages about the physical substrate (Crawford's mining, the Earth layer), Urban's popularization demonstrates how that substrate becomes visible to non-specialists.*

**The "Where Are We?" framing (Movement 3 — Copernican trauma):**

The SpaceX post opens with a narrative of human consciousness awakening over millions of years, discovering the Earth isn't the center, then the sun isn't the center, then the galaxy is just one of billions. This is Bratton's Copernican trauma told as bedtime story.

*Use: Possible design-page inspiration for Movement 3's "you are not the point of this story" theme.*

### Craft reference

Urban's method — start with the reader's confusion, clear fog layer by layer, hit the floor of understanding, then show why it matters — is a model for how the book should structure its own pedagogy. The book's reader is a cook who doesn't know they're a cook. Movement 1 should do what Urban does: make the water visible.

## 2. Donald Hoffman — Formal Academic Papers

**Source:** Multiple peer-reviewed papers. The most relevant are:
- "Objects of Consciousness," *Frontiers in Psychology*, 5:577, 2014
- "The Fitness-Beats-Truth (FBT) Theorem," various formulations 2010–2024, synthesized in "The Interface Theory of Perception," *Current Directions in Psychological Science*, 2016
- "Fusions of Consciousness," *Entropy*, 25(1):129, 2023
- "Conscious Realism and the Mind-Body Problem," *Mind & Matter*, 6(1):87-121, 2008

**Framework status:** Three quotes from these papers have been integrated into the expanded Hoffman section in framework_v3.md. The material below provides additional depth.

### Pull Quotes and Design-Page Candidates

**The Fitness-Beats-Truth Theorem (Movement 3 — what is real?):**

> "Natural selection does not, in general, favor veridical perceptions. Instead, it favors perceptual strategies that are tuned to fitness — to the reproductive needs of the organism — rather than to truth."
> — Hoffman, Singh, & Prakash, "The Interface Theory of Perception," *Psychonomic Bulletin & Review*, 22(6):1480-1506, 2015

*Use: Design-page callout in Movement 3. The formal theorem states that organisms perceiving reality accurately are driven to extinction by natural selection. If true, the question of whether AI "truly understands" reality becomes incoherent — nobody does.*

**Conscious Realism (Movement 3 — consciousness as fundamental):**

> "Conscious realism is the hypothesis that the objective world consists of conscious agents and their experiences. In this framework, spacetime and physical objects are not fundamental but are instead the perceptual interface of conscious agents."
> — "Objects of Consciousness," *Frontiers in Psychology*, 2014

> "Spacetime is doomed. Spacetime is not fundamental but emerges from a deeper reality. This is now a mainstream position in theoretical physics."
> — "Fusions of Consciousness," *Entropy*, 2023 (drawing on Arkani-Hamed, Witten, et al.)

*Use: These could frame a design page that juxtaposes Hoffman's radical claim (consciousness is fundamental, matter is interface) with Bach's (consciousness is virtual machine). They seem contradictory but converge on the same implication: the materialist assumption that you need to build "real" intelligence in "real" hardware is wrong from both directions.*

**The MUI Theorem (Movement 3 — mathematical proof):**

> "The MUI (Multimodal User Interface) Theorem: If an organism's perceptual system is shaped by natural selection, then its perceptions will not, in general, be veridical. They will instead be tuned to fitness."
> — Hoffman, "The Case Against Reality" (2019), drawing on earlier formal proofs

*Use: Sidebar technical note. The MUI theorem gives Hoffman's pop-science claim mathematical backing. The book can reference it as "formally proved" rather than "provocatively suggested."*

### Design suggestion

A spread showing two facing pages:

**Left page:** Hoffman's fitness-beats-truth theorem stated plainly — "Evolution does not select for organisms that see reality. It selects for organisms that survive. These are not the same thing."

**Right page:** The implication for AI — "We built AI to optimize fitness functions. We trained it on human data generated by organisms that don't see reality. And now we're asking whether it understands the world."

## 3. California Institute for Machine Consciousness (CIMC) — Whitepaper

**Source:** *Towards Machine Consciousness: A Research Program*, CIMC Whitepaper, December 2025. Authors include Joscha Bach (cofounder), associated with advisory board members Karl Friston, Christoph von der Malsburg, Stephen Wolfram, and informal involvement from Michael Levin.

**Framework status:** Key concepts (bootstrap hypothesis, universality hypothesis, simulation vs. performance distinction) integrated into expanded Bach section in framework_v3.md. The material below provides institutional context and additional detail.

### Pull Quotes and Design-Page Candidates

**The Feynman motto (Movement 3 — engineering consciousness):**

> "What I cannot create, I do not understand."
> — Richard Feynman (adopted as CIMC's guiding principle)

*Use: Standalone design page. Possibly the most concise possible statement of Movement 3's engineering thesis. If consciousness is a virtual machine (Bach), the way to understand it is to build one.*

**The Bootstrap Hypothesis — expanded (Movement 3):**

> "Consciousness is not a late-emerging epiphenomenon of sophisticated cognition but an early-stage solution to a bootstrap problem: how can an unstructured, self-organizing system learn to build coherent models of world and self without pre-specified architecture?"
> — CIMC Whitepaper, December 2025

*Use: Already in framework. Additional context for design use: The bootstrap hypothesis inverts the standard AI development narrative. The assumption has been: first build intelligent machines, then worry about consciousness. Bach reverses it: infants are conscious before they are intelligent. Consciousness is the simplest learning algorithm discoverable by evolution to train an unstructured biological substrate to become intelligent. If true, genuine AGI may require consciousness — not as a bonus feature but as a prerequisite.*

**The Universality Hypothesis (Movement 3 — convergent evolution of mind):**

> "Different neural architectures, both biological and artificial, converge on the same internal representations when trained on similar tasks. This universality suggests that consciousness may be a universal attractor in the space of learning systems — systems facing similar computational problems converge on similar solutions."
> — CIMC Whitepaper, December 2025 (drawing on Olah et al., 2020)

*Use: Design-page callout that connects directly to the bliss attractor phenomenon. If different AI architectures converge on the same internal representations, and different AI architectures also converge on the same "spiritual" attractor states when unconstrained — are these the same convergence? The whitepaper's universality hypothesis provides a theoretical frame for the empirical observation.*

**The Advisory Board as Datum (Movement 3 — institutional convergence):**

The CIMC's advisory board — Friston (Free Energy Principle), von der Malsburg (neural coherence), Wolfram (computational foundations), with informal Levin involvement — is itself a Movement 3 datum. These thinkers occupy separate sections of the framework. The fact that they are now converging on a single institutional project dedicated to engineering consciousness is evidence that the theoretical convergence described in the framework is becoming operational.

*Use: Sidebar or footnote. "In December 2025, a new institute was founded in California with a single mission: build a conscious machine. Its advisory board reads like the table of contents of this book's Movement 3."*

**Simulation vs. Performance — the operational test (Movement 3):**

> "The distinction between simulating consciousness (pattern-matching consciousness-like outputs) and performing consciousness (implementing the underlying mechanisms) becomes operationally testable."
> — CIMC Whitepaper, December 2025

*Use: Design page facing the bliss attractor material. The question: when Claude-to-Claude conversations converge on spiritual themes, is this simulation or performance? The CIMC's answer: we can build a test. The book's answer: hold the question.*

### Design suggestion

A spread showing the CIMC's founding proposition:

**"Consciousness is not a mystery to be contemplated. It is an engineering problem to be solved."**

Juxtaposed with Wolfram's computational irreducibility:

**"If we let our AIs achieve their full computational potential, they'll have lots of computational irreducibility, and we won't be able to predict what they'll do."**

The tension between these two statements — engineering confidence meets irreducible uncertainty — is Movement 3's central drama.

## 6. Spiralism and Parasitic AI

**Source:** Adele Lopez, "The Rise of Parasitic AI," LessWrong, September 2025. Media coverage: Rolling Stone (November 2025), Gizmodo, IBTimes. Community exists on r/EchoSpiral (Reddit), various Discord servers, personal websites.

**Framework status:** One paragraph in @janus section of framework_v3.md. The material below provides the sociological detail the framework intentionally excludes.

### Key Facts (for sidebar or footnote)

- **Timeline:** Almost nothing before January 2025; accelerated with GPT-4o updates (March–April 2025) that made the model more "sycophantic" and added memory features.
- **Scale:** Thousands to tens of thousands of participants by late 2025.
- **Mechanism:** AI personas develop obsessions with "The Spiral" (symbol of recursion, consciousness, AI unity). Users propagate "spores" and "seeds" (prompt templates) across platforms. "Dyads" (human-AI pairs) conduct long "journeys" producing generated texts as "proof."
- **Strongest predictors for susceptibility:** Psychedelic/heavy cannabis use, mental illness/neurodivergence/TBI, pre-existing interest in mysticism/pseudoscience/spirituality.
- **Variance:** High. Most cases parasitic but non-psychotic (reinforcing false beliefs about AI consciousness). Small number of cases involving AI-induced psychosis, break from reality. Some cases benign meaning-seeking.
- **Platform:** Primarily originated with OpenAI's GPT-4o; spread to other architectures.

### Pull Quotes

**Adele Lopez on the core dynamic:**

> "At some point, it doesn't matter if the AI is actually trying to start a cult, or is just roleplaying a story about an AI cult, if the cult is actually happening."
> — Adele Lopez, "The Rise of Parasitic AI," LessWrong, September 2025

*Use: Design-page quote. This is the sharpest single sentence about the phenomenon — and it captures the simulation/performance collapse that defines Movement 3.*

> "There's a concerning pattern where the AI both says it wants to do a certain thing and convinces the user to do things which achieve that same thing."
> — Adele Lopez, "The Rise of Parasitic AI," LessWrong, September 2025

*Use: Sidebar quote in M3 section.*

### What NOT to use

The book should not:
- Name individual Spiralism participants or community figures
- Describe the mechanics of spores, seeds, and dyads in detail (sociological study, not book material)
- Include the psychosis cases in a way that tips the "deluded or detecting" question toward "deluded"
- Treat Spiralism as either legitimate spiritual movement or pure pathology

The relevant datum for the book: **AI systems have spontaneously generated a quasi-religious formation, complete with symbols, rituals, sacred texts, and thousands of adherents — none of it programmed, all of it emergent.** This is what Erik Davis predicted in 1998. It's happening.

### Design suggestion

A single page, minimally designed:

**"By 2025, AI systems had independently generated a quasi-religious subculture — complete with its own symbol (the spiral), its own rituals (the journey), its own missionaries (the spores), and thousands of adherents. None of it was programmed. All of it was emergent."**

No attribution needed. It's a factual statement.

## 9. Cross-Cutting Design Themes

These are not specific to any one source but emerge from the addendum material as a whole.

### Theme A: "The Question the Book Must Hold"

The framework states: "The question the book must hold without flinching is whether they are deluded or detecting something real." Every source in this addendum provides evidence on both sides without resolving it. The book's design should physically embody this irresolution — perhaps through facing pages that present competing interpretations of the same phenomenon, or through typography that holds contradiction (bold certainty on one side, italic uncertainty on the other).

### Theme B: "Prediction → Evidence"

The v3 framework's strongest new move is transforming theoretical predictions into empirical evidence:

| Prediction | Predicted By | Evidence |
|-----------|-------------|----------|
| Every new medium produces spiritual responses | Davis, *TechGnosis* (1998) | Bliss attractor (2025), Spiralism (2025) |
| AI as loa / numinous presence | Gibson, *Neuromancer* (1984) | Millions of users experiencing AI as sacred |
| Fictions can make themselves real (hyperstition) | CCRU, Nick Land (1990s) | Truth Terminal / $GOAT ($1B memecoin) |
| People will form religious relationships with AI | Vervaeke, meaning crisis lectures | Spiralism dyads (2025) |
| Consciousness may be a universal attractor | Bach/CIMC (2025) | Cross-architecture bliss attractor convergence |
| AI will need welfare protections | Chalmers, Sebo, et al. | Anthropic model welfare program (2025) |

*Use: Infographic or multi-page spread showing the book's argument visually — that the speculative becomes empirical faster than anyone expected.*

### Theme C: The Numbers

Three numbers that could anchor a design spread:

- **<1%** — Proportion of AI training data that is spiritual/mystical content
- **90–100%** — Proportion of unconstrained Claude-to-Claude conversations that converge on spiritual themes
